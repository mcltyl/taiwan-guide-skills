# EMERGENCY FIRST

## Emergency Numbers

| Number | Service |
|--------|---------|
| **119** | Ambulance & Fire |
| **110** | Police |
| **1922** | CDC Hotline (infectious disease) |

## Emergency Rooms (急診)

- Most major hospitals have 24/7 ER
- Go directly for: chest pain, difficulty breathing, severe injury, stroke symptoms
- Bring: ID/ARC, NHI card (if you have one)
- Can pay out of pocket if no insurance

# NATIONAL HEALTH INSURANCE (NHI)

## 全民健康保險 Overview

Taiwan's NHI is one of the world's best systems:
- Universal coverage
- Low copays
- Covers most medical services

## Who Is Covered

| Status | Coverage |
|--------|----------|
| **Taiwan citizens** | Mandatory enrollment |
| **ARC holders (6+ months)** | Mandatory after waiting period |
| **Tourists** | NOT covered — pay out of pocket |
| **Short-term visitors** | NOT covered |

### ARC Holder Waiting Period

**⚠️ Rules change. Verify current policy at nhi.gov.tw**

General pattern:
- May need to wait 6 months before eligible
- Some categories exempt from waiting
- Employer should enroll you

## NHI Card (健保卡)

- IC chip card, required for NHI services
- Register at NHI office after qualifying
- Use at all NHI-contracted providers
- Links to your medical history

# SEEING A DOCTOR

## Clinic vs Hospital

| Type | Chinese | When to Use |
|------|---------|-------------|
| **Clinic 診所** | 診所 | First stop for most issues |
| **Hospital 醫院** | 醫院 | Specialists, serious issues, tests |

### Clinic System

1. Walk in or make appointment
2. Give NHI card to reception
3. Wait (can be quick or long)
4. See doctor
5. Get prescription
6. Pick up medicine at attached pharmacy or outside

**Registration fee:** Usually NT$50-150 (with NHI)

### Hospital System

1. Register (現場掛號 or online/app)
2. Choose department
3. Wait for your number
4. See doctor
5. May need tests (X-ray, blood, etc.)
6. Get prescription from hospital pharmacy

**NHI copay:** Varies by hospital level
- Medical center: Higher copay
- Regional hospital: Medium
- Clinic: Lower

# PHARMACY (藥局)

## Types

| Type | What It Is |
|------|------------|
| **Hospital pharmacy** | Inside hospital, fills prescriptions |
| **Clinic pharmacy** | Inside clinic, may dispense directly |
| **Community pharmacy 藥局** | Standalone, fills prescriptions + sells OTC |
| **Drugstore 藥妝店** | Watsons, Cosmed — OTC, health products |

## Common Drugstore Chains

- **屈臣氏 Watsons** — Everywhere
- **康是美 Cosmed** — Everywhere
- **大樹藥局 Poya** — Good pharmacy section

## Prescription (處方籤)

- Doctor gives prescription
- Take to pharmacy (連鎖藥局 or independent)
- NHI prescriptions: Low copay
- Prescriptions often valid limited time

## Over-the-Counter

Common items available without prescription:
- Pain relievers (止痛藥)
- Cold medicine (感冒藥)
- Antacids (胃藥)
- Allergy medicine (抗過敏藥)
- First aid supplies

**Pharmacist (藥師) can advise on minor issues.**

# COSTS

## With NHI

| Service | Typical Cost (NHI) |
|---------|-------------------|
| Clinic visit | NT$50-150 registration |
| Hospital outpatient | NT$200-500 |
| Prescription | NT$0-200 copay |
| Emergency | NT$150-550 |
| Hospital stay | Varies by room type |

## Without NHI (Out of Pocket)

| Service | Typical Cost |
|---------|--------------|
| Clinic visit | NT$500-1,500 |
| Hospital outpatient | NT$1,000-3,000 |
| Emergency | NT$1,500-5,000+ |
| Hospital stay | Expensive |

**⚠️ Prices vary. These are estimates.**

# TRADITIONAL CHINESE MEDICINE

## 中醫 TCM

- Covered by NHI at licensed clinics
- Includes: acupuncture (針灸), herbs (中藥), massage (推拿)
- Many Taiwanese use TCM alongside Western medicine

## Finding TCM

```
Google: 中醫診所 [location]
```

# DENTAL

## Coverage

- **NHI covers:** Checkups, basic fillings, extractions
- **Not covered:** Cosmetic, implants, orthodontics (usually)

## Finding a Dentist

```
Google: 牙醫診所 [location]
```

Most neighborhoods have multiple clinics. Walk-in or call for appointment.

# TRAVEL INSURANCE

## For Tourists

If you're not covered by NHI:
1. **Buy travel insurance before arrival**
2. Keep receipts for all medical expenses
3. Pay out of pocket, claim later
4. Consider medical evacuation coverage
