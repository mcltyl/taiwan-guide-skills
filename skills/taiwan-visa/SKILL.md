# VISA CATEGORIES

## Tourist / Visa-Free Entry

### How to Check If You Need a Visa

1. Go to: **boca.gov.tw**
2. Look for: "Visa-Exempt Entry" or "Visa Requirements"
3. Find your nationality
4. Note: Duration allowed, any restrictions

### General Framework (verify current rules)

| Category | Typical Duration | Notes |
|----------|-----------------|-------|
| Visa-exempt countries | 30-90 days | Most Western countries |
| Visa required | Varies | Apply at TECO before travel |
| eVisa eligible | Varies | Online application |

### What to Check

- ✅ Passport validity (usually 6+ months)
- ✅ Return/onward ticket requirement
- ✅ COVID-related rules (check if still applicable)
- ✅ Any recent policy changes

---

## Visitor Visa (停留簽證)

For stays beyond visa-free period or non-exempt nationalities.

### Types
- Single entry
- Multiple entry

### Where to Apply
- TECO (Taipei Economic and Cultural Office) in your country
- Or online (eVisa) if eligible

### Documents Typically Needed
- Passport
- Photo
- Application form
- Proof of funds
- Itinerary
- Purpose documentation

**⚠️ Requirements vary by nationality. Check BOCA website.**

---

## Resident Visa (居留簽證)

For long-term stays (work, study, family).

### Categories

| Purpose | Notes |
|---------|-------|
| **Employment** | Need work permit first |
| **Investment** | Business visa pathway |
| **Study** | Accepted by Taiwan school |
| **Family** | Spouse/dependent of citizen/resident |
| **Missionary** | Religious work |
| **Retirement** | Plum Blossom Card (seniors) |

### Process (General)

1. Obtain purpose document (job offer, school acceptance, etc.)
2. Apply for resident visa at TECO
3. Enter Taiwan
4. Apply for ARC within 15 days

# WORK IN TAIWAN

## Work Permit (工作許可)

### Key Principle

**Employer applies for work permit, not the employee.**

### Categories

| Type | Who |
|------|-----|
| **Professional** | Skilled workers, teachers |
| **Blue collar** | Manufacturing, caregiving (restricted) |
| **Specialist** | Tech, specific industries |
| **Gold Card** | High-skilled talent program |

### General Requirements (verify current)

- Job offer from Taiwan employer
- Qualifications (degree, experience)
- Salary threshold (changes periodically)
- Employer quota compliance

### How to Research

1. **Ministry of Labor** (mol.gov.tw) — Official requirements
2. **Workforce Development Agency** — Work permit rules
3. **Search:** `site:mol.gov.tw 外國人 工作許可`

---

## Gold Card (就業金卡)

Special visa for high-skilled talent — work permit + resident visa + open work rights.

### Eligible Fields (verify current list)

- Technology
- Science
- Economics
- Education
- Culture & Arts
- Sports
- Finance
- Law
- Architecture

### Benefits
- 1-3 year validity
- Open work permit (any employer)
- Tax benefits (first 5 years)
- Family can apply for dependent visas

### How to Apply
- **Portal:** goldcard.nat.gov.tw
- Online application
- Documentation of expertise

**⚠️ Criteria and benefits change. Check official portal.**

# ARC (外僑居留證)

## What Is ARC?

**Alien Resident Certificate** — Your ID card for living in Taiwan legally.

### Who Needs It
- Anyone staying 180+ days for work/study/family
- Must apply within 15 days of arrival on resident visa

### Where to Apply
- NIA (National Immigration Agency) service centers
- immigration.gov.tw

### Documents (General)

- Passport
- Resident visa
- Photos
- Proof of address
- Purpose documents (work permit, enrollment, etc.)
- Health check (from Taiwan hospital)
- Fees

### ARC vs APRC

| ARC | APRC |
|-----|------|
| Temporary residence | Permanent residence |
| Tied to purpose (job, etc.) | Independent status |
| Renewal required | No renewal needed |
| Restricted work rights | Open work rights |

# APRC (永久居留證)

## Permanent Residence

### General Requirements (verify current)

- 5+ years legal residence (or less with special qualifications)
- 183+ days/year in Taiwan
- Good conduct
- Financial stability
- Basic Chinese ability (or exemption)

### Special Categories

| Category | Reduced Requirement |
|----------|---------------------|
| Spouse of citizen | Shorter residence requirement |
| High-skilled | May qualify faster |
| Senior foreign professionals | Special pathway |

### How to Research

1. **NIA website:** immigration.gov.tw
2. **Search:** `永久居留 申請資格 外國人`
3. **Forums:** `site:ptt.cc 永久居留 APRC`

# CITIZENSHIP (歸化)

## Naturalization

### Key Point

**Taiwan generally requires renouncing original citizenship** (with some exceptions).

### General Requirements (verify current)

- 5+ years legal residence
- 20+ years old
- Good conduct
- Self-supporting
- Basic Chinese ability
- Renounce original nationality (usually)

### Exceptions to Renunciation

Some categories may retain original nationality:
- High-level professionals
- Significant investment
- Special contributions

### Process

1. Apply for naturalization certificate
2. Renounce original nationality (if required)
3. Apply for Taiwan ID (戶籍登記)
4. Apply for Taiwan passport

**⚠️ Citizenship rules are complex. Consult immigration lawyer for your specific case.**

# QUICK REFERENCE

## Acronyms

| Acronym | Full Name | What It Is |
|---------|-----------|------------|
| TECO | Taipei Economic and Cultural Office | Taiwan's de facto embassy |
| BOCA | Bureau of Consular Affairs | Handles visas |
| NIA | National Immigration Agency | Handles residence |
| ARC | Alien Resident Certificate | Resident ID card |
| APRC | Alien Permanent Resident Certificate | Permanent residence |
| MOL | Ministry of Labor | Work permits |

## Process Overview

```
Tourist: Just show up (if visa-exempt)
         ↓
Work:    Job offer → Work permit (employer applies) → 
         Resident visa (TECO) → Enter Taiwan → ARC (NIA)
         ↓
Long-term: Renew ARC → After 5 years → APRC eligible
         ↓
Citizen:  APRC → 5 more years → Naturalization eligible
```
